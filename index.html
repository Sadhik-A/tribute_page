Who is Kohli

Virat Kohli, a prominent figure in the world of cricket, is a dynamic and charismatic Indian cricketer whose remarkable achievements have solidified his status as one of the sport's modern-day legends. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, Kohli's journey from a young and ambitious player to the captain of the Indian cricket team is nothing short of inspiring. Known for his exceptional batting prowess, Kohli's elegant and aggressive style at the crease has garnered admiration from fans and experts alike. His ability to consistently perform across all formats of the game has earned him numerous records and accolades, including being ranked among the world's top batsmen.


Off the field, Kohli's passion for the game is matched by his commitment to various charitable endeavors and his active engagement in promoting social causes. He has used his platform to raise awareness about fitness, wellness, and the importance of leading a healthy lifestyle. Kohli's leadership both on and off the field has established him as a role model for aspiring cricketers and a source of inspiration for people across the globe. As he continues to shape the narrative of cricket with his exceptional skills and dedication, Virat Kohli remains a symbol of excellence and a true embodiment of the spirit of the game.


Virat Kohli's family is an integral part of his life, providing unwavering support and love throughout his journey as a cricket icon. Born to parents Saroj and Prem Kohli, Virat's upbringing in Delhi was marked by a strong foundation of values and encouragement. His family's dedication to his aspirations and dreams played a pivotal role in shaping his character and determination. In 2017, Virat Kohli tied the knot with Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, solidifying a union that garnered widespread attention and admiration. Their marriage exemplifies a harmonious blend of two accomplished individuals from different realms of entertainment and sports. Anushka's presence has not only added a touch of glamour to Virat's life but has also strengthened his support system. Together, they've become a power couple celebrated for their achievements and commitment to each other. Their shared journey showcases the importance of companionship and mutual respect in the pursuit of excellence, both on and off the field.


Achievements

2008

Virat Kohli made his debut for the Indian cricket team in an ODI against Sri Lanka in August 2008, marking the beginning of a stellar international career.

2010

Kohli was awarded the ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year for his outstanding performances with the bat and his significant contributions to India's success in limited-overs cricket.

2012

In 2012, Kohli became the fastest Indian to score 1,000 runs in ODIs and was awarded the prestigious Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna award, the highest sports honor in India.

2016

Virat Kohli was appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats, showcasing his leadership skills and commitment to the team's success.

2018

Kohli achieved the number one ranking in Test cricket, a testament to his consistent batting performances and ability to thrive in all conditions.

2019

Under Virat Kohli's captaincy, India won their first-ever Test series in Australia, a historic moment for Indian cricket.

2020

Kohli became the fastest player to score 12,000 runs in ODIs, further solidifying his reputation as one of the greatest ODI batsmen.

2021

Virat Kohli was named one of TIME magazine's 100 most influential people in the world, recognizing his impact both on and off the cricket field.

2022

Kohli reached the milestone of 75 international centuries, joining an elite group of players with this remarkable achievement.

2023

Virat Kohli announced his retirement from international cricket, concluding a remarkable career marked by numerous records and his undeniable influence on the sport.
